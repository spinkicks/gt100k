## Executive summary

This document stress-tests the GT100K PRD (v1.10) against its originating brainlift, using ~12 dispatched sub-analyses that argue each contested point from both sides. One thesis organizes it: **the PRD is a governance-first reconciliation of a governance-hostile brainlift, and it has blunted the edge of all five spiky bets — mostly correctly, but not always with the drift stated on the record.**

The single most important finding: **environment totality — the brainlift's declared #1 lever — is structurally undeliverable** in a non-boarding, non-surveilling day program (~⅓ of waking hours, none of the home), and cannot be recovered even in the R&D track (which also forbids surveillance). The honest move is to **rescope the headline thesis to "school + peer + culture" on the record.** On the other four SPOVs the direction is *recovering* fidelity through ethical mechanisms: SPOV 4 (breadth) is barely a betrayal (mornings are already ~100% spine); SPOV 5 (friction) is an upgrade if felt-friction is relocated to effort-gates + reward salience; SPOV 1 (family) is recoverable by separating the *dose* (keep) from the *gate* (reject); SPOV 3 (grouping) is recovered and even becomes the deliverable proxy for the lost home lever.

**The highest-leverage gaps are operational, not ideological.** The three potentially existential holes the PRD never addresses: **(1) guide supply** (~5,000 expert-generalists; the substrate the entire human-authority model rests on — solvable via a SPOV-3 near-peer grow-your-own ladder); **(2) safeguarding-at-scale + mandatory reporting + breach blast-radius for 100K children's records**; and **(3) the TimeBack single-point-of-failure + the date-driven Phase-0→1 engine cutover.** Close behind: no declared **legal operating model** (the full-day structure *is* school-replacement but claims none of the obligations), no **unit-economics / funding** model (~$0.7–1.7B/yr, ~80% labor), no **age-14 exit ladder**, and no **committed longitudinal eval** to test what is, by the PRD's own §5 taxonomy, an E3/R bet given production authority over a child's entire education (fixable near-free via a regression-discontinuity design at the Track A/B cutoff).

Reading guide: §0 the meta-thesis + five-softenings table · §1 the gap inventory · §2 the four SPOV debates · §3 seven load-bearing gap deep-dives · §4 the consolidated, compliance-hardened feature set · §5 the cross-doc fidelity audit (new gaps, G-dial compliance corrections, fidelity scorecard) · §6 the three existential operational-risk deep-dives (safeguarding, TimeBack, breach) · §7 the priority-ranked recommendation register (all 21 recs, tiered). **All of this is analysis, not a spec change** — adoption goes through the normal PRD/governance workflow, and the fixed G-class rights dials win over anything proposed here.

## 0. The meta-thesis: a governance-first reconciliation of a governance-hostile brainlift

The brainlift is deliberately *spiky*: it picks five positions each "false to current consensus, and true anyway," and it is unapologetic about the intensity elite results demand. The PRD (v1.10) is a **governance-first reconciliation** of that brainlift. In converting thesis into a buildable, defensible product, the PRD has *blunted the edge* of every one of the five SPOVs — sometimes as a necessary legal/ethical correction, sometimes (arguably) at the cost of the actual bet.

The single most important question for this refinement: **Is GT100K still the brainlift's program, or has it quietly become "an excellent, safe, well-governed gifted school" that dropped the spiky bets that were the whole point?** The brainlift's one-line summary is that *environment totality* is the dominant lever. The PRD forbids home sensing, runs a bounded school-day dose, and made contracts renewable. If the dominant lever is the home environment and the product can only touch 3–4 school hours, the central hypothesis may be structurally untestable inside the mainline product.

This is not a call to un-govern the product. The rights dials are correct and must hold. It is a call to notice *where the softening is load-bearing* and either (a) recover the brainlift's intent through an ethical mechanism, or (b) state plainly that the bet has been dropped and why — rather than letting the brainlift sit in the repo as a "foundation" the product no longer honors.

### 0.1 The five softenings (brainlift → PRD)

| SPOV | Brainlift ideal (spiky) | PRD (v1.10) reconciliation | Softening verdict (pre-debate) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1 — Select the family, not the child** | TV-free homes, a parent on the hook daily, **legally binding 8-yr continuation contracts**, psychological profiling of parents to predict who folds | Support-adjusted **renewable** partnership; irrevocable contracts **rejected**; family-execution model is **advisory only**, never a selection input; no home surveillance | **Heavy.** The selection mechanism the brainlift called the #1 lever is now a support program with an advisory nudge. |
| **2 — Gate on IQ ~120–125** | A hard cognitive floor, stated "without flinching," a full SD below the gifted-establishment line | Cognitive Floor Engine repositioned from **admission gate** to **placement/readiness**; admissions team gates on external CogAT; 120–125 is a *hypothesis to be validated* | **Light.** The floor idea survives; ownership moved and the number is (correctly) treated as unvalidated. Most faithful of the five. |
| **3 — Homogeneous grouping, ruthless competition** | Cohorts of 5–6, direct competition, rivalry "kills the dead weight of the slowest kid" | Cohorts of 6, near-peer; but competition is dose-metered, opt-in, anonymized, **no-bottom-rank**, non-harm floors, guide can remove it entirely | **Moderate.** Grouping kept; the *ruthless competition* engine is heavily de-fanged. |
| **4 — Specialize brutally early, BURN breadth** | Ages 6–14 on a narrow spine; "enrichment buffet deleted"; breadth is a cheap late-teen bolt-on | Program **protects breadth** ("strong foundations across the other fields the state/federal curriculum requires"); wildcard exploration floor; Spine Curriculum Generator's "breadth elimination" **rejected** | **Direct inversion.** The PRD does the opposite of the SPOV. This is the sharpest contradiction. |
| **5 — Friction is the product; make help hurt** | Refuse the answer; **decayed ELO reward** so shortcutting is "mathematically worthless"; discomfort built in on purpose | Answer-blind tutor kept; punitive decay **rejected**; replaced with **non-punitive potential-based** independence reward — "asking for help costs nothing" | **Contested.** Arguably a *cleverer implementation* of the same incentive (potential-based shaping is reward-equivalent), or arguably the removal of the "hurt" that was the point. |

### 0.2 The yield question (the sixth tension)

The brainlift's guardrail: "optimize for the students who thrive at full intensity and **route the rest into ordinary excellent schooling**." The PRD renamed "no-scrap" to **no-abandonment**, reframed routing as a **change-of-fit**, and committed an **intent-to-treat** yield denominator (all children ever enrolled; routed-out counted as non-attainment). This is more honest and more humane — but it also quietly concedes that a meaningful fraction won't reach the age-14 bar, which sits uneasily with a "100,000 enrolled reaching MIT-readiness" promise. The honest ITT number is the right call; the open question is whether the *program economics and marketing* have absorbed what that number will actually be.

## 1. Gap inventory (first pass — to be deepened by subagent debate)

Ranked roughly by how load-bearing the gap is.

1. **Environment totality has no delivery mechanism.** The brainlift's #1 lever is the total environment (home/peers/immersion). The PRD can only touch a 3–4 hr school block + afternoon, with *no* home sensing and renewable (not binding) family commitment. There is no product surface that delivers "immersion" outside school hours in an ethical, non-surveillance way. **This is the deepest gap** — the central hypothesis may be untestable in the mainline product. *(Candidate fix: §3 brainstorm — Home Environment Compact, peer-family geographic pods, sibling/parallel enrollment.)*
2. **Guide/mentor supply at 100K scale is hand-waved.** 1 guide per ~20 learners → ~5,000 guides who are "experts across a variety of technical fields, extensively trained." Recruiting, training, and retaining 5,000 expert-generalist guides is arguably a harder problem than any software system in the PRD, and it has no pipeline, no cost model, no fallback. *(Candidate fix: grow-your-own near-peer guide ladder — SPOV-aligned.)*
3. **Unit economics / who pays.** 100K learners × (expert guides + isolated Firecracker workspaces + LLM inference + equipment loans + expert minutes + family compensation) has no cost-per-learner, no funding model, no sustainability story. The brainlift mentions "my capital" and family "compensation" but the PRD is silent on money at scale.
4. **Age-14 exit / off-ramp is nearly absent.** "MIT-level readiness by end of 8th grade" — then what? At 14 the child is 3–4 years from college. There is no continuation credential, early-college bridge, high-school transition, or "what happens to a 14-year-old with AP Calc BC and no high school." The Passport exists but the *institutional* off-ramp does not.
5. **Legal schooling status is undefined.** Is GT100K a school (accreditation, diploma, teacher-of-record, compulsory-education/truancy compliance)? Or a supplement to enrollment elsewhere? The 3–4 hr academic block + afternoon implies it may *replace* normal schooling for 6–14 year-olds, which triggers a large body of education law the PRD never names.
6. **Content library production at depth.** A grade-by-grade content library is explicitly out of scope, yet the enrichment/olympiad track, the six-domain Interest Lab, and per-domain project ladders all require enormous authored content. TimeBack supplies core academics; the *rest* (passion domains, olympiad, adapters for software/research/robotics/startup/documentary/audio) is a content-production problem with no plan.
7. **The pool→100K funnel math is unreconciled.** "Filtering and selecting from a pool" (brainlift) vs. admissions-team-owned CogAT with Track A/B. What's the applicant pool size, Track A/B mix, and yield to hit 100K enrolled? Owned elsewhere, but the *program* depends on the number and never states it.
8. **Cohort-of-the-floor pressure question.** SPOV 3 wants rivalry to manufacture elite pressure. But a cohort of six students *all* at the 120–125 floor may not generate the "rivalry a one-on-one tutor can't fake" — you may need variance/spikes in the cohort. The compiler matches on pace/velocity, not on the presence of a pace-setter. Unexamined.
9. **Twice-exceptional (2e) intensity interplay.** Glossary defines 2e; the accommodation rules are strong; but the *interaction* of full-intensity dose with a co-occurring disability (when accommodation and intensity pull opposite directions) is under-specified.
10. **Multi-year outcome/eval design.** The whole thesis is unfalsifiable in the 4-month synthetic build. There's no committed longitudinal evaluation design (cohorts, comparison groups, pre-registration) for the 8-year outcome — so the program could run for years without ever testing its own central claims.

### 2.1 The Breadth Betrayal (SPOV 4) — *resolved: mostly a mislabel, one real conflict*

**Verdict: the PRD did not betray SPOV 4 in the mastery block; it under-states the alignment and over-protects one perpetual floor.** The apparent inversion conflates two different "breadths."

- **Steelman brainlift:** SPOV 4's warrant is a *resource* claim, not a taste — "breadth is cheap to bolt on in the late teens once the machinery exists; the years of cheap automaticity never come back." Concentrated early practice is how every objective-elite field is entered (Ericsson et al. 1993; Polgar). Automaticity only compounds if scarce childhood hours are concentrated, not sprayed. On this reading the PRD's enrichment track, 6-domain Interest Lab, compulsory-breadth clause, and *permanent* 10–20% wildcard floor each leak the program's rarest input.
- **Steelman PRD:** Primary intake is ages 6–8 — too young to know the spine; a mis-narrowed six-year-old *is* the foreclosure §14.1 forbids. The breadth clause is **compulsory-curriculum compliance** (a legal boundary, not a preference). Option value is load-bearing: the wildcard is insurance against betting eight irreversible years on the wrong spine. Assent gates specialization (G-class) — you cannot burn breadth before a child can assent to the spine.
- **The real disagreement (narrow):** The PRD's **morning is already the SPOV-4 spine** — §12 defines exactly two required spines (quant + verbal), 90%-gated, with enrichment explicitly *additive and non-gating*. The §14.7 percentages allocate only the **afternoon**; mornings are ~100% spine. So daily narrowing is far higher than the table implies. Genuine breadth lives in two places that must be told apart: (a) **breadth of exploration probes** (Interest Lab + wildcard) — which a narrow-spine advocate should *endorse* as option-value reconnaissance ("you can't specialize on the *right* spine without first finding it"); and (b) **breadth of foundational subjects** (compulsory curriculum + any co-equal enrichment) — the only head-on conflict, where the brainlift is right that unrecoverable hours are spent but the PRD legally cannot delete it.
- **Synthesis recommendation (buildable):**
  1. **Name the spine as the spine** in §12/§2.1 — state that the morning mastery block *is* the thesis's narrow quant+verbal spine and that automaticity in these years is the program's scarcest non-recoverable input (the reason breadth is bounded, not co-equal).
  2. **Reclassify the two breadths in §14.7** — label Interest Lab + wildcard as *option-value scouting / no-foreclosure insurance*, front-loaded (scout then commit), and make the **wildcard floor decay with age and spine-confidence** (consistent with the existing 20%→15%→10% taper) rather than a permanent buffet. Prefer *spine-adjacent* wildcards so exploration compounds the spine.
  3. **Compress compulsory breadth to the legal minimum**, scheduled *outside* automaticity-critical drill hours, and label it in §2.1 a compliance floor, not a mastery target.
  4. **Hold the G-dials fixed** — assent-gated specialization, no model-driven foreclosure, non-penalized accommodations. These convert "specialize early" into "narrow *as fast as the child's own validated evidence and assent allow*," honoring "cheap automaticity years don't come back" without betting a six-year-old on an adult's guess.
### 2.2 The Friction Softening (SPOV 5) — *resolved: an upgrade, but the PRD over-claims the theorem*

**Verdict: reward-equivalent in optimal policy, materially weaker as a *felt* deterrent — a deliberate, defensible trade. But the PRD should not lean on the potential-based-shaping (PBRS) theorem as if it guaranteed behavioral equivalence. It does not.**

- **The technical claim:** The independence reward is genuinely potential-based in *form* — R ≈ Φ(s′) − Φ(s) on the mastery-estimate potential (Ng, Harada & Russell 1999). The invariance theorem is real: it leaves the *optimal policy* unchanged, so for an idealized value-maximizer "solve unassisted" dominates under both the PRD reward and the brainlift's decayed-ELO tax; both make a post-rescue submit near-worthless. They diverge on the **experienced gradient**: (1) *Sign floor* — the PRD reward is floored at ~zero and never removes what's earned; the brainlift's decay *subtracts* from visible standing. Under loss aversion (losses loom ~2×), "you lose X" is a stronger deterrent than "you gain 0" even when a rational agent ranks the policies identically — so the punitive form more strongly suppresses the help button, which was the brainlift's literal goal. (2) *Perception* — PBRS invariance is an asymptotic idealization for a rational RL agent; children respond to felt salience, and §14.13 concedes the 6–8 band "cannot yet read" the mastery-delta currency. The steering signal is the one the youngest learners can't perceive.
- **Steelman brainlift:** Desirable difficulty (Soderstrom & Bjork) and productive failure (Kapur) only work if difficulty is *felt and not bypassed*. A costless help button + invisible, deferred, floored-at-zero reward means a low-self-regulation 6–8-year-old hits help reflexively. Kosmyna (cognitive offloading) is invoked as a design spec: discomfort is the intended brake. Remove the hurt, remove the brake at the moment it's most needed.
- **Steelman PRD:** Punishment *suppresses help-seeking*, which is dangerous (a stuck child who won't ask stalls or hides confusion); adaptive help-seeking is a metacognitive skill to cultivate, not tax. Loss-framed streaks/decaying meters are dark patterns banned by §14.12.5 (effectively G-class). Competence-contingent reward experienced as *informational* supports intrinsic motivation (Deci/Koestner/Ryan); a punitive tax is the *controlling* frame that crowds it out and triggers fixed-mindset withdrawal (O'Keefe/Dweck/Walton).
- **Synthesis recommendation:** Felt friction can be preserved *without* punishment or loss-framing, because **desirable difficulty lives in the task structure, not the reward's sign**:
  1. **Effort-gate help, don't tax it** — strengthen the §13 attempt-before-hint ladder; the "cost" of help is real cognitive effort/time, not lost points (felt but non-punitive, G-safe).
  2. **A reflective speed-bump before escalation** ("what have you tried?") as the explicit anti-offloading brake — a System-2 pause, never a countdown, never gating accessibility/safety help.
  3. **Gate the salient reward *object* behind unassisted work** — for 6–8, render friction as an *earned* "I did it myself" marker; its absence is a *gain not yet obtained*, not a loss, giving young children a perceivable approach-motivated signal the mastery-delta number can't. Closes the §14.13 perception gap.
  4. **Make struggle status-bearing** (§14.12.4) so the hard path is a chosen approach goal, not a costless default.
  - **PRD should add:** a sentence in §13/§14.12 stating felt friction comes from *effort/process gates and reward salience, not reward sign* (so no future reader reintroduces decay under SPOV-5 pressure); an acknowledgment that PBRS invariance is an idealization and the *behavioral* deterrent is carried by §14.12/§14.13, not the reward math; and a **help-seeking-rate guardrail** that treats *suppressed* help-seeking as a failure signal — which operationalizes the PRD's divergence from the punitive design and makes the whole dispute empirically testable.
### 2.3 The Family-Selection Retreat (SPOV 1) — *resolved: recover the dose, permanently reject the gate*

**Verdict: the PRD can recover the brainlift's *dose* intent in full while permanently rejecting its *gate* mechanism. Fanaticism is welcomed when a family chooses it; it is never a filter the program applies to families.**

- **Steelman brainlift:** The one-line thesis is that environment dominates, and SPOV 1 locates the decisive variance in "the far tail of parental obsession, exactly what the [twin-study] data never sees" — a range-restriction argument, with Polgar as existence proof. If "a merely able child in a fanatical home crushes a gifted child in a lukewarm one, every time," then screening *out* fanaticism (or refusing to screen *for* it) forfeits the largest untapped source of variance. The brainlift treats family fidelity as a selection *gate* co-equal with the cognitive floor.
- **Steelman PRD:** The instruments — irrevocable 8-year contracts on minors' families and covert "who folds" psychological profiling — collapse under the fixed G-dials. Binding contracts make exit costly exactly when a child wants out (the coercion G1/G3 exist to forbid; assent must be revocable). Fold-prediction encodes protected-class proxies (wealth, structure, personality) that §2.3/§10 ban and the subgroup false-decline audits would fail. TV-free-home verification and daily-availability monitoring require the home sensing categorically prohibited by §10.2/G6. **Advisory support (RF-04) is the only lawful form.**
- **The key distinction:** separate **(a) selecting families** — ranking/excluding households on predicted fidelity (prohibited) — from **(b) composing and supporting the family environment** post-eligibility (permitted, and where the real dose lives). The brainlift's actual goal is *environmental dose delivered*, not *devotion measured at intake*. Dose can be manufactured after admission through **voluntary, revocable intensity** instead of contracts and profiling.
- **Synthesis recommendation (buildable, all G-dials fixed):**
  1. **Voluntary "intensity ladder" surface** in Family OS — named, revocable rungs (daily load, difficulty band, specialization depth) with per-dial child assent (§8.2), so committed families opt *up* with no contract and no inferred escalation; lowering never requires justification; devotion is never scored (FC-01).
  2. **Family-cohort clustering (new, RF-07):** the Household Schedule Compiler groups compatible committed families into peer pods so shared cadence and norms replace psychological screening — explicitly non-ranking, non-excluding.
  3. **Sibling / parallel-enrollment path:** documented in §10 as a dose amplifier (one household's buy-in → multi-child immersion), gated only on each child's independent readiness and veto (G2) — never a household-loyalty discount.
  4. **Transparency-first commitment funnel:** codify the compensated shadow trial (§10) as the *only* commitment signal — measuring follow-through against *declared* availability and recovery after disruption, not attendance perfection — feeding RF-04 Advisory support, never a gate. This is the ethical substitute for fold-prediction: fanaticism *revealed by the family's choice*, not predicted by profile.
  5. **Explicit anti-proxy reaffirmation** in §2.3/§10: TV-free homes, parental availability, and household structure are never eligibility inputs; the program buys dose with support and self-selection, not screening.
### 2.4 Environment Totality without Surveillance — *resolved: rescope the headline thesis, honestly*

**Verdict: environment totality in the Polgar sense is not achievable for a non-boarding, non-surveilling day program, and GT100K should stop implying it is. The honest move is to rescope the mainline thesis to "school + peer + culture environment" on the record — not to park the home claim in the R&D track (which cannot test it either).**

- **Frame the gap:** A 6–14-year-old has ~13–15 waking hours/day. The program touches ~5–8 program-hours on weekdays (3–4h academic + 2–4h studio), sensing confined to school premises. Across a week that's ~30–40 program-hours vs. ~95–100 waking hours — **roughly one-third of waking life, and effectively zero on evenings/weekends** — precisely the home/family/unstructured zone that produced the Polgar result, and precisely where §10.2/G5/G6 (correctly) forbid all sensing. The PRD already quietly rescoped SPOV 1 ("converts family selection into support-adjusted partnership," §34) without updating the headline thesis to match. *That mismatch is the real gap.*
- **What's ethically recoverable (no home sensing, ever):**
  - **Peers (strongest lever — presence observed at school/shared spaces, never sensed at home):** geographic **peer-family pods** — weight the Cohort Compiler (§15) on *residential proximity* so cohort families cluster and after-school peer density rises organically; cross-family culture via cohort rituals and cross-school collaboration (already supported, functional-data-only). This uses SPOV 3's homogeneous grouping — "the biggest lever in the building" — as the deliverable proxy for the home lever we cannot pull.
  - **Family agency (self-authored, self-reported, never sensed/scored):** extend `FamilyPlan` (RF-01) + Household Schedule Compiler into a **family-authored home-rhythm compact** — the family declares its *own* rituals (protected reading time, TV-free windows echoing SPOV 1, shared-project evenings); Family OS *supports* the rhythm the family chose and never verifies it (FC-01 forbids scoring devotion). **Sibling/parallel enrollment** is the highest-density ethical amplifier: the enrolled children *are* the immersion vector, carrying the field home voluntarily, with zero new sensing.
  - **Culture/immersion as *portable* norms:** the language of the field, shared artifacts, the child-owned Passport (§21), public events (§20) — immersion the child carries into their own time without the program observing it.
- **The honest alternative:** Do **not** relocate the home-totality claim to the §31.1/G8 radical-dose track — G8 is explicitly bounded by §23 and forbids surveillance *even under research consent*, so it cannot test Polgar-style home totality either. Parking it there would be a dodge implying "awaiting evidence" when the mechanism has been ruled out *on principle*. Use G8 narrowly for what it legitimately can study — *dose and rivalry intensity within consent* — not as a hiding place. The honest statement: **GT100K bets on school + peer + culture environment, deliberately declines home totality on rights grounds, and treats the family's role as self-authored partnership, not program-controlled immersion.**
- **Synthesis recommendation (top 3):**
  1. **Rewrite the environment thesis in the brainlift and PRD §1/§2.1 to "school + peer + culture environment," explicitly and on the record** (cross-link §10.2, G5/G6). Single most important fix — aligns the headline lever with what the product can deliver, matching the honesty already in §34.
  2. **Build residential-proximity weighting + peer-family pods into the Cohort Compiler (§15)** and a **family-authored home-rhythm compact into `FamilyPlan`/Family OS** (RF-01, §10) — the maximum ethical recovery of peer density and family immersion.
  3. **Make sibling/parallel enrollment a first-class product path** (enrollment handoff §3.5 + Family OS) — the only mechanism that raises *home* immersion without any sensing.

## 3. Load-bearing gap deep-dives (batches 2–3)

### 3.1 The 5,000-guide problem — the unpriced human bottleneck (gap #2)

**Verdict: guide supply is not one risk among many — it is the substrate the entire human-authority governance model stands on, and the brainlift's own near-peer logic supplies the pipeline the PRD lacks.**

- **The math:** §32.4.1 plans ~1 guide per 20 learners (250 guides at the 5,000-learner ceiling). Linearly to 100K → **~5,000 guides, ~200 psychometricians, ~240 safeguarding, ~480 mentor-ops** *before attrition*. But §6.3's guide is a STEM-literate, safeguarding-trained, in-person expert-generalist "available at all times" — far rarer than a subject teacher, drawn from a market with chronic STEM shortages. At ~15%/yr turnover, steady state burns ~750 guides/yr; the ramp plausibly needs **8,000–10,000 gross hires to net 5,000 filled seats, indefinitely.** And 1:20 is a *floor*: §6.3 drops the ratio for younger cohorts and active interventions, and 6–8s (primary intake) are high-touch.
- **Why load-bearing:** Every consequential decision needs a *named human* (governance/constitution); §18 promises "a human available at all times." If guides don't exist in number, those guarantees are hollow and the tutor's escalation-to-human path dead-ends. §32.4.1 *already* freezes enrollment when safeguarding <2 or caseload >25 — the PRD has conceded thin staffing is a stop-the-program condition, but only built the breaker for the beta.
- **The grow-your-own ladder (SPOV-3 aligned):** the brainlift makes near-peer teaching *the engine*, and "guide not teacher" means most labor is coaching/facilitation — work advanced learners can do.
  - **Tier 0 — near-peer cohort tutor** (advanced learner +1–2 levels): retrieval drilling, worked-example walkthroughs, first-line "stuck" triage, belonging. SPOV-3 peer pressure formalized, nearly free.
  - **Tier 1 — graduate apprentice guide** (18+ alumni): assists a lead guide on a training track; alumni already embody the eight-year environment and cost far less than open-market hires.
  - **Tier 2 — guide** (§6.3 role): safeguarding-cleared, named, holds decision authority.
  - **Tier 3 — lead/senior guide + scoped external mentors.**
  - **Authority limit:** a near-peer (minor or uncleared junior) owns *facilitation and encouragement only* — never the 90% mastery gate, dose, safeguarding, or interventions. Every cohort always has a cleared **adult** Tier-2 guide reachable; near-peers extend reach, never replace the named-human floor. Safeguarding for minors-mentoring-minors: on-platform-only channels, bounded age-gap cap, two-adult supervision, logged like `MentorAssignment` (EM-04).
- **Recommendation:** (1) new **§6.4 "Staffing pipeline & guide tiers"** codifying the ladder + the authority reservation; (2) **age-band ratios** replacing flat 1:20 — ~1:12 (6–8), ~1:18 (9–11), ~1:22 (12–14), active-intervention learners double-weighted, plus a near-peer coverage target per cohort; (3) a **steady-state staffing model** extending §32.4.1 to 100K with gross-hire + attrition + alumni-supply math; (4) **promote the freeze rule from beta to a standing enrollment governor at all scales**, with graceful degradation (pause *new*-cohort formation first, never abandon active learners — consistent with v1.10 no-abandonment), and a Release Threshold Registry row for **guide-pipeline health** (fill rate, attrition, alumni-conversion) owned by workforce ops.

### 3.2 The age-14 exit and legal-schooling-status gaps (gaps #4, #5)

**Verdict: the full-day two-halves structure functionally *is* school-replacement, yet the PRD claims none of the obligations that triggers; and the hard age-14 stop strands the graduate at the exact moment the brainlift says the payoff arrives.** *(US law specifics below are approximate and jurisdiction-dependent — confirm with counsel per operating state.)*

- **The exit problem:** §2.5 hard-ends the program at 14 and then falls silent on what the child *does* with AP 5s + SAT 1570. The only exit machinery is the §21 Passport (proves competencies, confers no admission/enrollment/diploma) and a "humane transition record" on withdrawal. Four options: **early college / dual enrollment** (strongest fit — the Passport is built to feed a "university verifier," but the PRD builds no articulation, no bridge; the credential points at a door the program never opens); **a GT high-school continuation** (coherent with acceleration but contradicts the age-14 end + renewable-agreement language); **radical acceleration into university** (the SMPY/Lubinski-Benbow lineage the brainlift explicitly invokes — the option the evidence base most directly endorses, yet unnamed); **return to conventional high school** (wasteful — re-sitting mastered material is the "dead weight" SPOV 3 rejects, and GT100K grants no accredited transcript).
- **The legal-status problem:** The PRD never states whether GT100K is a school; **IDEA and FERPA appear only in the glossary** — acronyms, not obligations. Three models: **(i) full-time school replacement** (triggers compulsory-attendance law, private-school registration/licensure, teacher-of-record/certification, diploma+accreditation authority, and critically **IDEA/§504 child-find + IEP** duties the PRD's admissions-side psychometric accommodations do *not* satisfy); **(ii) supplement/enrichment** (lightest load, but impossible — a 3–4h morning block + 2–4h afternoon studio *is* the school day, leaving no room for conventional school); **(iii) homeschool-support / microschool / hybrid** (parents remain legal educators; offloads attendance + diploma duties onto families; fits the Family OS framing; per-state registration varies).
- **Which the structure implies:** the full-day intensive can't be (ii), so GT100K functionally operates as (i) or (iii) — but it runs a full-day school while remaining *legally undefined*, which is not internally consistent.
- **Brainlift tension:** SPOV 4 spends 6–14 narrow *precisely so* breadth bolts on "in the late teens" — a claim about after 14. The whole thesis points at continued acceleration into college-level work, not a stop. Ending at 14 with a maximally accelerated, minimally broad child and no bridge contradicts the program's own rationale; the horizon should extend to a college bridge.
- **Recommendation:** (1) new **§22 "Exit Ladder and College Bridge"** — early-college/dual-enrollment partnerships with named articulation targets that consume the Passport; an optional 1–2 yr SMPY-modeled bridge cohort reconciled with the renewable-agreement language (extends, not violates, the age-14 boundary); a documented conventional-school re-entry path with credit-mapping; and exit-outcome metrics (placement rate, credential-acceptance rate) added to §2.6. (2) new **§0-level "Legal Operating Model"** statement declaring per-jurisdiction which model (i)/(iii) applies, made policy-as-code-visible (§29); if (i), commit to accreditation + teacher-of-record + diploma authority + an explicit **IDEA/§504 child-find + IEP** obligation (closing the glossary-only gap). (3) **Interaction:** only an accredited school (i) issues a portable *diploma*; a microschool (iii) makes the Passport the *sole* credential, raising the stakes on university-verifier acceptance and making early-college articulation the load-bearing off-ramp. The PRD must pick a model *because* it determines which exit is buildable.

### 3.4 Content production at depth + the pool→100K funnel (gaps #6, #7)

**Verdict: the Practice-Item Foundry covers the academic *spine only*; the afternoon layer is human-authored and gated by *review throughput, not generation velocity*. And 100K forces heavy Track B reliance, which moves the real selectivity tension from the ability floor (SPOV 2, internally consistent) onto family selection (SPOV 1, governance-barred).** *(Numbers marked [EST] are estimates — the PRD supplies none.)*

- **Content supply:** The Foundry works for the spine because §12.3 makes generation **fail closed** without a verifier/rubric/calibration record, and math/reading items have answer keys, measurable DIF, and post-hoc calibration. The **afternoon layer has none of these preconditions** — Interest Lab probes measure work-mode preference and voluntary return (not correctness), projects yield open-ended artifacts, the olympiad track needs expert proof-judgment. So the generative approach does *not* transfer. Authored surface [EST]: PASS-002's 6 domains × 6 work modes × 2 difficulty × audience/no-audience × equivalent variants (×3–4) × ~3 age bands ≈ **~1,300–1,700 authored, safety-classed probe variants**, plus 6 project ladders and 6 Foundry adapters (each with MilestoneContracts, verifiers, rubrics, risk-class gates). **The bottleneck is human review, and it does not get the 50x:** AI can generate candidates fast, but §14.4.1 safety-class + §16 approval-owner gates + §12.3 fail-closed mean nothing child-facing ships until a human reviews it — and for open-ended/physical-risk content, review is the *whole gate*, not sampling. Live content is capped at review throughput, not generation throughput.
- **Funnel:** US ages 6–14 ≈ ~36M [EST]. IQ ≥120 is +1.33 SD → top ~9% ≈ ~3.3M floor-eligible [EST]; Track A (~top 2%) ≈ ~730K; Track B (120–130 band) ≈ ~2.6M. To net 100K *enrolled* under the ITT rule + trial attrition (~20–40% shortfall → ~130–160K offers), a defensible **applicant pool of ~0.5–1.5M** (ITT yield ~7–20% applicant→enrolled) [EST]. **None of this appears in the PRD or ADMISSIONS_PRD.** Critically, **Track A alone cannot fill 100K** (~14% of every top-2% US child would have to enroll) — 100K *forces* heavy reliance on Track B.
- **Brainlift tension:** SPOV 2 *deliberately* sets the floor a full SD below the gifted line and admits "the entire band the gifted programs throw in the trash" — so **100K at IQ~120 is internally consistent with SPOV 2**; the ability gate is intentionally near-non-selective at the top tail. The real tension is with **SPOV 1**: the built system selects on the lever the brainlift calls *weak* (child CogAT score) and is *governance-barred* from selecting on the lever it calls *strongest* (family fidelity — RF-04 advisory only, trial post-eligibility/non-selective). At 100K with a low floor and no family gate, the thesis's selectivity has nowhere to live — the program risks becoming "wide pool, low floor," a large below-cutoff cohort selected on ability the thesis itself says is insufficient. *(This is the funnel-side confirmation of §2.3's conclusion: "elite" must rest on environment/dose, not selection.)*
- **Recommendation:** (1) new **"Content Supply & Authoring" section (§12.x)** naming an accountable owner, stating explicitly that Foundry generation covers the **spine only** and the afternoon layer is human-authored + human-safety-reviewed, publishing the ~1,300–1,700-variant estimate and **separate "generation velocity" vs. "review throughput" capacity lines** (the latter caps live content), and extending fail-closed to afternoon content; (2) a **Funnel-Assumptions Appendix** (§3.5 / cross-ref PIPELINE-PRD) stating the program's dependency — required pool size, Track A/B mix (with the honest finding that Track B must carry the majority), attrition, ITT yield — logged as an explicit admissions+recruitment risk; (3) **reconcile SPOV 1 on the record** — state plainly that family fidelity is *deliberately not* an admission gate (a knowing divergence from the brainlift) and that the floor is IQ~120 ≈ top ~9%, not the gifted line, so "elite" rests on environment/dose, not selection; put the honest numbers in §2.5/§2.6.

### 3.5 Cohort-of-the-floor pressure — does near-peer matching engineer out the rivalry? (gap #8)

**Verdict: SPOV 3 welds two different mechanisms into one sentence; the PRD's *standings surface* preserves the frontier well, but its *compiler objective* endangers it. Fixable without touching the non-harm floor.**

- **Two mechanisms, not one:** "matched pace kills the dead weight of the slowest kid" is a **floor** operation (remove drag); "rivalry and shared advance generate a pressure no tutor can fake" is a **ceiling** operation (a visible upward target that redefines what's possible). They're orthogonal — a cohort can have neither, either, or both. The brainlift's own exemplars (Polgar; every objective-elite field) are **spike/frontier** stories, so its evidence base leans *heterogeneity-with-a-pace-setter* while its cohort prescription ("five or six matched students") reads as pure variance compression. Productive social comparison needs a *slightly-upward, attainable* referent — which structurally **requires some dispersion**.
- **What the PRD optimizes:** the caliper is "level-**plus-velocity**" and the compiler "value[s] close pace" — literally compressing variance on *both* axes, including the velocity axis the visible standings reward. **Steelman "spike engineered out":** tight level+velocity calipers make gain-based standings rank trivial noise; no durable frontier; a flat, low-energy cohort with neither dead weight nor pace-setter. **Steelman "frontier preserved":** visible standings rank *gain, not level*, are sprint-reset, and surface "the child's own gain against the band's **top**, not 'last of N'" — so the frontier *rotates* (whoever gains fastest this sprint is the spike, contestable next sprint) and is an aspirational display with the demoralizing bottom suppressed by design. Honest read: **the standings surface preserves a frontier (Reading B); the compiler objective endangers it (Reading A)** because "value close pace" doesn't distinguish "compress level" from "compress velocity."
- **Brainlift fidelity:** the non-harm floor + no-bottom-rank protect exactly the learner SPOV 3 wants to "cut" — but this is a **correct ethical fix that keeps the lever intact**: "kill the dead weight" describes a *mixed room*, and the PRD already dissolved the mixed room by calipering the floor, so there's no in-cohort dead weight to cut. The brainlift's cut happens at the *program* level ("route the rest"), which v1.10 reframed as change-of-fit + ITT. The PRD relocates the cut from *in-cohort humiliation* to *admission fit* and keeps matched pace — a softening of SPOV 3's *rhetoric*, not its *mechanism*. The one genuine fidelity loss is the velocity-compression risk (an optimization defect, not an ethics defect).
- **Recommendation — "Compressed Floor, Live Ceiling":** change the compiler objective from *compress all variance* to *compress level, tune velocity dispersion to a productive band*:
  1. **Replace "minimize velocity variance" with a productive-dispersion term** — constrain within-cohort velocity spread σ_v to a target band [σ_lo, σ_hi], penalizing *both* extremes (below → flat/no pace-setter; above → unreachable/discouraging). The single load-bearing change.
  2. **Frontier-guarantee constraint** — require ≥1 member whose projected sprint gain is top-quartile (a plausible pace-setter) *and* no member who can never reach the frontier across a rotation window (contestability + no-abandonment together).
  3. **Spikes on the velocity axis, never level** — gain-based, sprint-reset standings mean a slower-*level* child can be the velocity spike: generates aspiration *and* directly serves no-abandonment (the "slowest" can lead the frontier). Can't become a fixed-ability caste (§15 prohibition holds).
  4. **Cross-cohort frontier import** — use the existing opt-in, anonymized, near-peer-band cross-cohort standing to show a locally-flat cohort the matched band's gain frontier — an aspirational tier with no within-room loser. Buildable on components already specified.
  - No-abandonment invariants unchanged (non-harm floor hard; belonging as rollback gate; "gain vs band top," never "last"; turn-off without penalty).

### 3.6 Longitudinal falsifiability — the program is an E3/R bet on real children with no committed causal eval (gap #10)

**Verdict: the program is a coherent stack of five empirical bets, yet the PRD commits to no design that could show any of them false. §33.2 honestly concedes the ceiling ("cannot claim the build caused an age-14 outcome") but never converts that honesty into a plan to eventually test the thesis on the children it enrolls.**

- **The SPOVs as falsifiable claims:** **SPOV 1** (family fidelity predicts attainment) — *partly* testable via RF-04-signal↔ITT-attainment correlation, but the causal core (totalizing vs. lukewarm home) is **ethically untestable** (can't randomize a child to a lukewarm home). **SPOV 2** (floor at 120–125) — **testable within program** via the Track A/B boundary; §33.1 already stages "validate, remap, raise, or reject" the range. **SPOV 3** (grouping is the biggest lever) — within-program randomization of *reversible composition* already contemplated (§15 "randomized neighbor swaps"), but the "biggest lever" ranking needs cross-program comparison. **SPOV 4** (specialize early) — **effectively untestable internally** (the whole program *is* the narrow spine; no breadth arm), needs cross-program comparison, confounded with selection. **SPOV 5** (friction) — **fully testable internally**: A/B reward framing + the attempt-before-hint/help-receipt guardrail are reversible choices measured by §33.2's retention/transfer suites.
- **The missing eval architecture (ethical constraint: can't withhold an admitted child's program):**
  - **Regression-discontinuity at the CogAT cutoff — nearly free, most powerful.** Admissions routes at a sharp cutoff (Track A above, Track B below); *both tracks are enrolled and receive the program*. Comparing age-14 outcomes just-above vs. just-below is a clean RD test of the SPOV-2 floor with **no child withheld**; the platform already receives the eligibility determination + rule version (§3.5), so the running variable exists.
  - **Within-program randomization of reversible choices** under the §8.5 bounded-automation envelope (reward framing, cohort composition, in-cap dose titration) — never the intensity floor itself.
  - **Staggered-enrollment / waitlist** across waves (sequenced, not denied); **matched-comparison cohorts** vs. other gifted programs for the cross-program SPOVs (1, 3, 4); **pre-registration** of each SPOV's predicted effect before data matures.
  - **GT-Twin (§31) is NOT a substitute** — synthetic populations, no production write credential, only shortlists a policy a human pilot must validate. Simulation cannot falsify a bet about real children.
- **The ethics-vs-evidence tension:** by §5's own taxonomy the thesis is largely **E3/R** — yet §5 says E3 gets "shadow/reversible pilot" and R gets "*no production authority*," while **the whole program is an E3/R bet given production authority over a child's entire education**. §5's rule ("delivery speed does not change an evidence class") should bind the *thesis*, not just individual models. The program earns intensity only through equipoise (which §33.1's floor-remap language concedes), staged intensity (§14.8 + dose caps), honest ITT accounting, and **stopping rules — which exist for exposure (§20.1) but not for the SPOVs themselves.**
- **Recommendation:** (1) new **"Longitudinal Evaluation & Falsifiability" section** as a first-class deliverable parallel to §33; (2) **pre-registered SPOV predictions with stopping rules** in the Release Threshold Registry (operational claim + outcome metric + predicted band + a change-the-program rule if it fails, mirroring §33.1's floor-remap); (3) **commit the RD design at the Track A/B cutoff** as the primary causal instrument for the floor (needs only a §3.5 data-sharing addendum to retain the running variable + outcomes); (4) **commit within-program randomization** of reward framing / composition / in-cap dose under §8.5; (5) **commit matched-cohort / staggered comparisons** for the cross-program bets, and **flag SPOV 1-causal and SPOV 4 as assumptions, not findings**; (6) **an independent evaluation body** (like the psychometric board) empowered to pre-register, hold data, and trigger stopping rules so the SPOVs can't ossify into dogma; (7) **bind §5 to the thesis** — because the central bets are E3/R, the program owes the missing E1/E2 evidence on its own children, and continued intensity is conditional on that evidence accruing.

### 3.7 Twice-exceptional (2e) at full intensity — access vs. intensity accommodation (gap #9)

**Verdict: every accommodation the PRD specifies is an *access* accommodation (changes *how* work is received); none is an *intensity* accommodation (changes *how much/how hard*). When the dose — not the content or channel — is the barrier for a floor-clearing gifted child, the PRD is silent, and serving 2e well is *required by SPOV 2*, not a concession to it.**

- **The interaction problem:** CF-03's set (large-print, screen-reader, extended-time, motor, language, quiet-environment) all change the channel; none changes the load. Even §12.1's 15–45 min block overrides only resize a block. Concrete conflicts: an **autistic** child clears the floor but rivalry-dose/churn are *structural cohort inputs* it can't toggle; an **ADHD** child's "extended time" *lengthens* blocks (often the wrong direction — the fix is more/shorter/fewer blocks, a dose reshape the schema can't express); **anxiety** meets the intended discomfort of attempt-before-hint/productive-failure (framing the surface doesn't lower the friction dose); **dyslexia** — a screen reader restores access but the verbal spine at full pace *is* the load, and the DIF audit catches measurement bias, not harmful volume/pace. In every case, "accommodate access" leaves the load fixed.
- **Back-door exclusion is real (via attrition, not the floor):** full-intensity default + no intensity accommodation → wellbeing-pulse/14-day flags → deload → "change-of-fit" route-out. The audits don't catch it: §12.1/CF-05 test *measurement* fairness, and being routed out for dose-intolerance is not a measurement error, so it never trips the freeze; disability/2e isn't a named approved subgroup. The honest ITT accounting makes aggregate loss visible (good), but the Equity row has **no disability/2e cut**, so 2e under-attainment dissolves into the general routed-out pool — the number exists, the harm isn't named.
- **Brainlift fidelity — serving 2e *is* SPOV 2:** the floor is set a full SD low precisely to "admit the entire band the gifted programs throw in the trash." A floor-clearing 2e child who *looks* high-friction is the paradigm discarded case. SPOV 2 gates on ability, not presentation-polish, and indicts confusing "a high starting line with the only one that finishes." Dropping a 2e child for being high-friction is gating on smoothness — the exact error SPOV 2 attacks. The "route the rest" guardrail is scoped to those *below the ability frontier*; a 2e child is *at* the frontier on ability with an orthogonal dose barrier. Routing them out conflates dose-intolerance with ability-deficit — the confound SPOV 2 forbids.
- **Recommendation (G-dials fixed):** (1) new **"Twice-Exceptional (2e) Learners"** subsection (§11.2 or §14.x) stating the access-vs-intensity distinction; (2) define **"intensity accommodation"** — an individualized, human-owned **dose ceiling** as a first-class accommodation-profile field (reduced focus-block count, capped rivalry-dose, reduced churn, slowed escalation, extended 14-day windows), implemented as a *per-learner override of the §33.1 population dose caps* and a new *cohort hard constraint* alongside individual-non-harm; (3) **extend the exemption rule** — a dose reduction never lowers mastery credit, standing, independence reward, or ITT attainment classification (parallel to access help always being exempt); (4) **fairness instrumentation** — add "2e/disability" as an approved CF-05 subgroup, a §12.1 audit cut, and an explicit line in the §2.6 Equity row *and* the ITT attainment metric; (5) **route-change gate** — for a floor-clearing 2e learner, a route-out requires evidence a *reduced-dose configuration was offered and failed on its own terms*, human-owned; (6) **reconciliation** — full intensity stays the default and population optimization target (frontier preserved for the modal child); the intensity accommodation is a strictly more-protective *individualized floor* only for children who clear the cognitive floor but not the dose default — consistent with v1.10's "G-class dials move only more-protective," and reading "route the rest" as SPOV 2 intends (route those who can't clear the floor, never those who clear it but need a different dose shape).

## 6. Operational-risk deep-dives (batches 4–5)

*Deepening the eight gaps the fidelity audit (§5.1) surfaced but only sketched — §6.1–6.3 the three "potentially existential" ones (batch 4), §6.4–6.7 the residual four (batch 5). All figures are order-of-magnitude estimates; child-protection and privacy law is jurisdiction-specific.*

### 6.1 Safeguarding at 100K + mandatory reporting (gap G-A)

**Verdict: the disclosure caseload at 100K is a low-hundreds specialist org the PRD hasn't staffed, and the home-sensing paradox resolves cleanly via a *disclosure ≠ sensing* bright line — but both need to be written down.**

- **Caseload [EST]:** rough US base rates → maltreatment reasonable-suspicion events ~1–4%/yr (1,000–4,000), youth suicidality disclosures ~1–5%/yr (1,000–5,000), other safeguarding ~1,000–3,000 → **~3,000–12,000 disclosures/yr (~10–40/day)**. At ~5,000 serious cases × ~3 active weeks → **~290 concurrently active cases**; at 25–40/specialist that's ~7–12 case-managers of *active-management* capacity *alone*, before 24/7 intake, cross-timezone on-call, leave, and a mandated-reporter support desk for ~5,000 guides. Linear scaling of §32.4.1's "12 on duty at 5,000" → 240 on duty at 100K; with round-the-clock + leave multipliers, a **total safeguarding org in the low hundreds (~200–400+)**. The current "freeze below two staff" trigger is calibrated to the 5K synthetic wave and does not scale.
- **The mandatory-reporting machine (absent today):** personal mandated-reporter status attaches to all ~5,000 guides+mentors (criminal/civil exposure the platform *cannot* discharge for them) → onboarding certification, jurisdiction-specific training, an in-product reporting-decision aid that never *blocks* an independent report; a triage/escalation workflow (guide→safeguarding→CPS/law enforcement) with the 15-min ack as *acknowledgement not resolution* and hard statutory-deadline timers (often 24–48h); contemporaneous quote-the-child documentation with chain-of-custody + a **legal-hold path distinct from routine crypto-shred**; a jurisdiction service resolving the rule by the *child's residence/where harm occurred* (50-state variation); good-faith-reporting protection held alongside the no-proxy and correction-rights dials; auto-escalation to the §30 incident commander for imminent-danger/on-program-actor cases.
- **The home-sensing paradox — resolved by a bright line:** *sensing* = the program instrumenting the home to extract data the child didn't volunteer (forbidden, G5/G6); *disclosure* = the child's own account to a trusted guide (a §6.3 duty, and the child exercising voice §8.2 protects). The no-home-sensing ethos forbids *reaching into* the home; it cannot forbid *acting on what a child chooses to tell us* — mandatory reporting makes that a legal duty. So safeguarding is **the one zone that may hold home-origin, family-identifying, legal-identity-linked content** (names, addresses, CPS case numbers) — quarantined in its own DB role/KMS key/audit stream, legal-hold-exempt from routine shredding, one-way-firewalled from admissions/mastery/motivation/sensitive-signal stores. Intake-only from the child, never an outbound sensing capability.
- **Recommendation:** new **GOVERNANCE "G9 — Safeguarding Operations & Mandatory Reporting"** (rights/legal class) + a companion PRD §30.x software workflow; a **ratio-based staffing rule** (case-managers per 1,000 enrolled + 24/7 on-call + guide-support desk) with a per-specialist active-caseload ceiling that fires an enrollment freeze, and an explicit ~low-hundreds 100K target; a **mandated-reporter framework tied to the legal-operating-model choice** (§3.2); a **CPS/law-enforcement interface spec** (severity tiers, legal-hold chain-of-custody, jurisdiction routing, incident-commander auto-escalation); the **disclosure-vs-sensing framing written into G5/G6/G7**; and acceptance tests (synthetic disclosure → triage → report within statutory deadline; jurisdiction gate; safeguarding hold pauses conflicting workflows; zero cross-zone leak).

### 6.2 TimeBack single-point-of-failure + Phase-0→1 cutover (gaps G-C, G-D)

**Verdict: the brainlift's "proven engine off the shelf" is a sound *build-time economy* but the PRD converted a *pedagogy* argument into an unhedged *vendor* argument — and the date-driven engine swap is the one place the PRD lets a calendar drive a load-bearing, child-facing gate.**

- **Dependency risk:** "pedagogy is solved" says the *technique* is a commodity; it does not say a *single partner's proprietary implementation* is a safe sole source. Phase 0 runs the entire academic spine on one external engine, with the whole surrounding product built "on that assumption." Unenumerated failure modes: price/terms (never stated, no contractual floor), quality drift (no version pin on GT100K's side), availability (an outage takes down the morning line for every learner, no degraded mode), reward-model change (GT100K depends on re-pointing the reward, but TimeBack can alter its XP substrate underneath), and — critically — **TimeBack is an external processor of child academic data (`AcademicSignalEvent`) with no DPA, sub-processor list, deletion path, or breach-notification chain named anywhere.** For a repo whose constitution treats child-data governance as G-class, that is a material gap.
- **Engagement-vs-difficulty conflict:** TimeBack is engagement-optimized ("pays out per focused minute"); SPOV 5 defines GT100K *against* exactly that. In Phase 0 the differentiating guarantees (answer-blind tutor, help receipts, independence reward, fail-closed provenance, per-skill DIF audit) are all *deferred* because the partner engine "does not implement these by design," and GT100K "asserts no §12–§13 guarantee for the academic block during Phase 0." So every Phase-0 learner runs on an engine that *contradicts the central pedagogy differentiator*, with the near-90% gate demoted to advisory. Reconciliation is only a "pre-cutover governance item" **with no fallback if irreconcilable** — if TimeBack's reward can't be re-pointed, GT100K has spent months delivering a product whose central claim was never live, with no engine that can deliver it but the still-unbuilt in-house one.
- **Cutover risk:** §12 makes it explicit — a *hard scheduled* swap, "not a per-skill or per-cohort fade" — and it's under-specified in the dangerous places: **no dual-run/shadow comparison, no mastery-state migration/reconciliation, no rollback criteria.** The one guardrail (clear the Phase-1 fairness audit before the gate becomes a hard lockout) audits the *new gate* but doesn't reconcile *migrated mastery state* (a learner mid-spine on TimeBack has no defined mapping into the PFA/BKT engine). If Phase 1 slips, the "advisory" TimeBack gate keeps governing real placement while the calendar pressures a swap onto an engine whose audit may not be clean — in direct tension with §32.4 ("cannot waive a gate to reach 5,000 learners by a date") and §25 ("AI-native speed accelerates building, never the decision to put something in front of a child").
- **Recommendation:** (a) new **§12.4 "Partner-dependency & contingency"** — a **DPA requirement** as a *blocking* condition for any live Phase-0 use (sub-processor list, purpose limitation, deletion/portability, breach SLA, via G7); version-pinning of the gate/content/reward contract; an availability **degraded-mode** (offline/cached block); an **exit plan** (mastery-history egress so `MasteryState` can be reconstructed); and an **in-house-accelerate / second-source trigger** (repricing beyond X, quality/DIF regression, or an un-re-pointable reward change auto-escalates Phase-1 priority), with an explicit *irreconcilable* branch that time-boxes Phase 0. (b) **Replace the hard date swap** with a **gate-driven cutover**: dual-run/shadow (both engines score the same learners; compare gate decisions + subgroup error before authority moves), mastery-state migration + per-learner reconciliation report with discrepancy hold, staged per-cohort rollout with the fairness-audit-before-hard-gate rule applied per cohort, and rollback criteria; the near-90% gate stays advisory until *both* the audit clears *and* reconciliation passes. (c) **Cutover happens when dual-run parity + reconciliation + DPA + per-skill fairness all pass — never because a date arrived**, closing the G-D tension.

### 6.3 Data-breach blast radius for 100K children's records (gap G-B)

**Verdict: prevention is genuinely strong; detection/response/recovery for a *mass* breach is near-absent — and the Identity account is a catastrophic single point of failure whose compromise collapses the entire seven-zone architecture at once.** *(Estimates flagged.)*

- **The Identity SPOF:** the architecture is a pseudonymity system — downstream services get only a pseudonymous reference; the identity service alone maps it to legal identity. But FOUNDATION §4.3 places in *one account* the identity vault, consent ledger, *and* the crypto-shred KMS keys. Compromise there does two compounding things: (a) it **re-identifies the entire population** (every learning/wellbeing/sensitive-research/eligibility record is keyed to references that become names), and (b) it lets the attacker **decrypt the off-graph payloads those same keys protect** — so one breach collapses six zones' pseudonymity *and* their encryption simultaneously. Worse than the sum of the zones breached separately. Joined via a re-identified crosswalk, the sensitive-research (prosody/gaze/rPPG features), wellbeing (distress/rest/bullying), and eligibility (cognitive-readiness) zones form a **per-child cognitive-and-emotional dossier**. EvidenceGraph integrity is "anchor-conditional, not intrinsic" — a store compromise can *forge* provenance, not just leak.
- **Crypto-shred doesn't help against a live breach:** it's an *erasure* mechanism (destroy a key so retained data becomes unrecoverable); against a live compromise the keys are by definition live and in use. The docs' strongest privacy control is orthogonal to mass breach.
- **What's missing vs. prevention:** no **incident-response playbook** (§30's incident commander does *child-safety* response — freeze room, disable contact — not data-breach containment); no **child-specific breach-notification workflow** (G7 names the COPPA/FERPA/state duty but no workflow, deadline mapping, subject enumeration, or templates exist); no **population-scale breach red-team** (§30 red-teams per-vector, not an aggregate Identity-compromise or bulk-exfil at 100K); no **key-compromise recovery / re-pseudonymization plan**; no **liability/insurance line** anywhere in the cost posture.
- **Child-specific severity:** subjects are 6–14 and cannot consent; harm is *lifelong and latent* (a re-identified minor can be misused for years, irreversibly); the exposed categories are the most sensitive the program recognizes. A mass breach **inverts every G-dial** — it exposes wholesale exactly what per-dial assent, veto, and sensitive isolation were built to protect.
- **Recommendation:** (1) new **"Incident Response & Breach" section** (FOUNDATION §14 / PRD §30) — severity tiers, a data-breach incident commander *distinct* from the child-safety IC, a child-data breach runbook, forensic-retention logging, fail-closed containment; (2) **Identity SPOF hardening** — split the identity↔pseudonym crosswalk from the consent ledger; move crypto-shred KMS keys under separate key-admins from Identity operators; HSM-backed keys with split-knowledge/quorum; break-glass four-eyes; rate-limit + anomaly-detect bulk re-identification reads; forbid any bulk-export path from the vault; (3) **breach-notification Temporal workflow** (mirroring the §13 deletion workflow) enumerating affected subjects and mapping COPPA/FERPA/state deadlines; (4) **population-scale breach red-team as a pre-live gate** in the Release Threshold Registry beside the §19.2 D1–D6 gates (aggregate exfil sim, Identity-compromise tabletop, key-compromise recovery + re-pseudonymization drill) — block live enrollment until passed; (5) **breach liability + cyber-insurance in the cost model** — *[EST]* at ~$150–300/record, 100K children ≈ **~$15–30M raw exposure before penalties**, plausibly the program's single largest liability and currently $0 in the stack.

### 6.4 Guide/staff wellbeing — the missing adult analogue to G4 (gap G-E)

**Verdict: the program meters *child* dose token-by-token but runs ~5,000 adults at full intensity with no dose meter at all; the §32.4.1 freeze triggers are all *lagging* supply metrics that fire only after the people are already gone.**

- **The asymmetry:** G4 gives children nine measure-families each with a required interpretation and privacy floor; the ~5,000 guides who run the program get nothing comparable. A guide is safeguarding-bearing, in-person, expert-generalist, "available at all times" at 1:15–1:25 (tightening to ~1:12 for 6–8s), each personally carrying a mandated-reporter duty (G-A) — yet the only staff signals anywhere are §2.6's "staff queue age" and "cost per learner" (throughput and cost, not the human). §14.10 even bars diagnosing burnout from telemetry for *children*; there's no adult equivalent because there's no adult monitoring at all.
- **Why load-bearing:** guide supply is the substrate of the human-authority model (§3.1); burnout is *endogenous* to the max-intensity design and drives the ~15%/yr attrition (~750 guides/yr) that hollows out the named-human floor. §32.4.1 concedes thin staffing is a stop condition, but every trigger (caseload >25, backup <15%, safeguarding <2) is a *lagging* supply metric — by the time caseload breaches 25, the people have already left. There is no *leading* measure of the guide state that produces the attrition.
- **Brainlift fidelity:** the ethical asymmetry *is* the fidelity point — the intensity ethos cannot silently exempt the adults *carrying* the friction. Unmeasured adult dose is a hidden softening of the human-authority floor, dressed as attrition.
- **Recommendation:** new **GOVERNANCE "G4b — Staff wellbeing and protected capacity"** (peer to G4) + a PRD §6.6 workforce hook — protected rest, a caseload-stress/safeguarding-exposure monitor (disclosure volume per guide, ties G-A), an autonomy/belonging staff pulse, and a **workload dial** (adult counterpart to the child intensity ladder). Import G4's telemetry-humility rule verbatim (self-report only, no diagnosis-from-telemetry, a declined pulse is missing data never adverse). Make burnout a **release-gate metric** (leading indicators in the Release Threshold Registry, not just caseload) and extend the §32.4.1 freeze to pause *new-cohort formation first* (no-abandonment). Add a **"Workforce" scorecard group to §2.6 with a paired guardrail** so throughput/cost pairs with staff wellbeing — which disciplines §3.3: since labor is ~80% of cost and guide ratio is a dominant cost knob, a wellbeing gate makes that ratio **non-cuttable** (the rest/backup/sub-25 floor becomes a fixed cost, not an optimization target).

### 6.6 Allocation-equity audit for scarce opportunity goods (gap G-G)

**Verdict: the program bans "caste via ranking" (G1/G6 + the §12.1 gate DIF audits) but leaves "caste via access" entirely unguarded — the three scarcest, most advantage-conferring goods are allocated by objectives with *no subgroup-equity term*, and one runs as live automated allocation.**

- **The gap:** every existing guardrail sits on the *mastery gate*. The scarce *opportunity* goods have none: the §12 enrichment/olympiad track is "additive… does not gate," yet is an unequal good (extra tail-extension for those who clear the core gates); the §16 Foundry broker schedules compute/equipment/lab-windows/expert-minutes via CP-SAT with "dominant-resource fairness" — but DRF equalizes across *resource types* to prevent starvation, **not across child subgroups**; the §18 EM-01 matcher ranks experts with no equity term, and §8.5 runs mentor-attention allocation as *bounded automation*. So the three most advantage-conferring goods are allocated by objectives containing no subgroup-equity term, one of them a live automated allocator.
- **Why load-bearing:** this is the unguarded path to the exact caste G6 forbids. If the same subgroups (G4 audit groups + §14.5 `family_context` resource advantage) repeatedly win olympiad slots, expert minutes, and warm compute, the program rebuilds durable stratification *through opportunity* — invisible to every existing audit because none tests allocation. §2.6 names "access to costly domains" but as a dashboard line, not a threshold bound to the allocators.
- **Brainlift fidelity:** an access-caste betrays SPOV 2 and 3 — it re-sorts the admitted band by home-resource advantage, the very range-restricted variable SPOV 1's *gate* was correctly barred from using (§2.3). Guarding access keeps "elite" resting on the program's dose, not on which child arrived with more.
- **Recommendation:** (1) **Opportunity-Allocation Equity Audit** (new §16.x/§18.x block extending the §12.1 framework from the gate to enrichment enrollment, expert minutes, and Foundry compute/equipment) — monitor *cumulative allocation share by approved subgroup* as a hard §2.6 Equity metric; (2) **fairness constraint on the allocators** — a subgroup-share guardrail band in the §16 CP-SAT objective and EM-01 ranking; because mentor-attention runs at bounded automation, a breach **auto-demotes the allocator to Advisory** (human-owned) pending repair, mirroring the §12.1 gate-freeze; (3) **equalize by supply, never by rationing** — extend the §8.3 cost-equalization duty (shared kits, remote labs, loaners, regional partners) to enrichment/mentor/compute so under-served subgroups get *more* access (enrichment stays additive/non-gating — the guardrail is on distribution, never on making enrichment a gate); (4) **name it in GOVERNANCE** — add to G6 a prohibition on *opportunity hoarding* (durable subgroup stratification of scarce opportunity goods even absent any ranking surface — the access-analogue of the banned caste leaderboard).

### 6.7 Terminal path for the perpetually-parked-passion child (gap G-H)

- **Why load-bearing:** a real dead-end the no-abandonment/change-of-fit language hides. Under the §2.6 ITT denominator this child silently becomes non-attainment, dissolved into the general pool — the same dishonesty §3.7 flagged for 2e. §14.10 forbids "automatic exclusion or forced continuation," so leaving the case undesigned means the program has no *lawful* move for it.
- **Brainlift fidelity:** SPOV 4 wants brutal specialization and routes non-thrivers "into ordinary excellent schooling"; an in-program **guided-generalist track** is the ethical form of "route the rest" that never forecloses the late bloomer (§14.1 forbids foreclosing a six-year-old; §14.5 `REOPENED` keeps the door open).
- **Recommendation:** (1) **terminal default state** (§14.5/§14.7) — when no `ACTIVE` spine persists across N park→reopen cycles, the afternoon defaults to a *sustained-exploration / guided-generalist* track built from the adjacent + wildcard paths §14.7 already retains — never an empty Build line; (2) **alternative masterpiece route** (§2.5) — extend the existing "individualized target horizon" precedent so the Independent-construction dimension is satisfied via a guide-scaffolded generalist capstone or a portfolio of smaller multi-cycle defended works, preserving the *construct* (multi-cycle, independent, defended) without requiring a durable passion; `REOPENED` into a deep spine stays available anytime; (3) **honest accounting** — track "no-durable-spine" as a named subgroup in the Passion + Masterpiece scorecards and the ITT metric, and treat repeated park→reopen cycling as a wellbeing/design flag; (4) **new §14.10 row** — "Perpetually parked / no durable spine → after N cycles, default to guided-generalist afternoon + alternative masterpiece route + subgroup tracking; never automatic route-out or forced continuation."
