# Interest Lab — AI 3D-World Generators as an Offline Content Pipeline

**Question:** Can we author beautiful, explorable "discovery zone" environments (a music studio, a coding lab, an art gallery…) *offline* with an AI 3D-world generator, then render them *at runtime in a web browser on school laptops/Chromebooks* (modest integrated GPUs, WebGL — not WebGPU-only)? Synthetic data only; output must be commercially usable in a shipped product for kids (~8–14), ideally a warm, kid-friendly stylized look.

**Short answer:** Yes — as a **hybrid** pipeline. Generate the environment "shell" offline with a world model, export to a **compressed Gaussian-splat or glTF mesh**, render it in-browser with a **WebGL2 splat renderer (Spark / SuperSplat)**, and hand-assemble the *interactive* props and zone logic on top (Spline or plain three.js). No single generator gives us a finished, interactive, license-clean, Chromebook-cheap zone by itself. Details, per-tool assessment, and licensing landmines below.

---

## TL;DR recommendation

- **Top pick — turnkey: [Marble](https://marble.worldlabs.ai) (World Labs).** Cloud generation (no local GPU), multimodal input, spatially-consistent *explorable* worlds, exports splats + meshes, and — critically — the same company ships **[Spark](https://sparkjs.dev/)**, an MIT-licensed WebGL2 splat renderer built to run these worlds on low-power devices. Commercial rights require the **Pro** plan. This is the fastest path to a shippable zone.
- **Top pick — cheapest + cleanest license: [Blockade Labs Skybox AI](https://www.blockadelabs.com/).** 360° stylized panoramas (48+ styles, incl. cartoon/anime), **full commercial license on every paid plan**, and a skybox renders as a textured sphere on *any* Chromebook. It's "look-around," not "walk-around," so use it for backdrops/skies + hand-built foreground (hybrid).
- **Top pick — own-the-pipeline / open-source: [HY-World 2.0](https://github.com/Tencent-Hunyuan/HY-World-2.0) (Tencent).** SOTA open weights, quality comparable to Marble, supports **cartoon/game styles**, exports splats + meshes. Flags: custom community license (**not valid in EU/UK/South Korea**; >1M MAU needs Tencent approval) and **multi-GPU** generation.
- **Interactivity layer: [Spline](https://spline.design/).** Not a full-scene world model, but a **native web 3D runtime** with clean commercial licensing and an inherently stylized/low-poly look that is the lightest thing to render on a Chromebook. Best tool for the actual clickable "discovery" mechanics.
- **Avoid as primary generators:** **WorldGen** and **LayerPano3D** (both depend on **FLUX.1-dev — non-commercial** → licensing blocker), **Luma AI** (reality-capture, realistic not stylized, conflicts with "synthetic only"), **CAT3D** (unreleased/proprietary), **Genie 3 / Oasis** (real-time *server-side video* world models — they don't export a portable asset and can't run on a Chromebook).

## Why the constraints reshape the shortlist

Two hard constraints do most of the filtering:

1. **"Render at runtime in a browser on a modest Chromebook (WebGL, not WebGPU-only)."** This kills the 2026 "headline" models — **Genie 3** and **Oasis** generate the world *frame-by-frame on a server GPU*; there is no file to download and nothing a Chromebook could render locally ([Genie 3](https://deepmind.google/blog/genie-3-a-new-frontier-for-world-models/), [Oasis](https://decart.ai/oasis)). It favors generators that emit a **static, portable asset** — a Gaussian splat (`.spz`/`.sog`/compressed `.ply`), a **glTF mesh**, or a **360° panorama** — which a WebGL2 renderer can stream and draw. On integrated GPUs, cheapness ranks: **skybox sphere < stylized glTF mesh < Gaussian splat (with LOD/streaming)**.

2. **"Commercially usable + synthetic only."** This is where several otherwise-great tools fall down. The recurring landmine is **FLUX.1-dev**, whose license is explicitly **non-commercial** ([BFL license](https://github.com/black-forest-labs/flux/blob/main/model_licenses/LICENSE-FLUX1-dev)). Any generator whose default panorama stage is FLUX.1-dev inherits that restriction (**WorldGen**, **LayerPano3D**). "Synthetic only" also rules out **Luma**, which digitizes *real* places.

### The browser-runtime layer (the enabling tech)
Whatever we generate, rendering is a solved-enough problem for Chromebooks in 2026 **if we keep the asset budget in check**:

- **[Spark](https://sparkjs.dev/)** (World Labs, **MIT**) — three.js splat renderer targeting **"98%+ WebGL2 support"** and "fast even on low-powered mobile devices" ([repo](https://github.com/sparkjsdev/spark), [Spark 2.0](https://www.worldlabs.ai/blog/spark-2.0)). Supports `.spz`, `.sog`, compressed `.ply`. **This is the recommended runtime.**
- **[SuperSplat Viewer](https://github.com/playcanvas/supersplat-viewer)** (PlayCanvas) — WebGPU renderer with **automatic WebGL2 fallback** and **streamed LOD** with a per-device "splat budget," so a scene too big for a phone still displays and refines progressively ([details](https://radiancefields.com/supersplat-ships-compute-based-webgpu-rendering-and-automatic-streamed-lod)). Good insurance for larger zones.
- **Rules of thumb for integrated GPUs:** prefer **compressed formats** (SPZ/SOG ≈ 5–10× smaller than raw PLY), keep splats in the low **millions** with LOD, and for the weakest hardware **export a stylized glTF mesh instead of splats** — meshes are the cheapest thing three.js/Babylon can draw.

## Per-tool notes

### Marble — World Labs *(top turnkey)*
Input: text, image, video, or coarse 3D layout. Output: **Gaussian splats (SPZ/PLY), collider + high-quality textured meshes (GLB), 360 panos, and video**; worlds are **editable, expandable, and combinable** and are spatially consistent enough to *walk through* ([blog](https://www.worldlabs.ai/blog/marble-world-model), [splat export](https://docs.worldlabs.ai/marble/export/gaussian-splat), [mesh export](https://docs.worldlabs.ai/marble/export/mesh)). Generation is cloud SaaS (credit-based) — **no local GPU**. **License:** exporting needs a paid plan; **commercial rights come with the Pro plan** (or API/Enterprise) — the Standard plan exports splats/collider meshes but **excludes commercial rights**, and Free can't export ([plans](https://docs.worldlabs.ai/marble/support/account-billing), [ToS](https://www.worldlabs.ai/terms-of-service)). **Runtime:** World Labs built **Spark** specifically to render Marble worlds in-browser → best-in-class fit for our Chromebook target. **Look:** photoreal-leaning; push it toward our kid-friendly style by feeding stylized reference images. **Verdict:** fastest, lowest-ops route to a beautiful explorable zone; budget for subscription + per-world credits and keep everything on Pro/API for commercial use.

### HY-World 2.0 — Tencent (+ WorldStereo) *(top open-source)*
Input: text / single image / multi-view / video. Output: **3DGS, mesh, point cloud, depth, normals, camera params**, via a four-stage pipeline (HY-Pano 2.0 → WorldNav → **WorldStereo 2.0** → WorldMirror 2.0) with first-person navigation and collision ([repo](https://github.com/Tencent-Hunyuan/HY-World-2.0), [paper](https://arxiv.org/html/2604.14268v1)). The paper reports quality **"comparable to the closed-source model Marble."** It explicitly generates **"realistic, cartoon, game"** styles → good for kid-friendly. **Hardware:** WorldMirror runs on a **single 12–24 GB GPU**, but full generation is heavier and **WorldStereo-2 is ~17B params → multi-GPU (FSDP)** ([hardware guide](https://docs.clore.ai/guides/3d-generation/hunyuan-world-2)). **License:** the standalone [WorldStereo](https://github.com/FuchengSu/WorldStereo) repo is Apache-2.0, **but the shipped HY-World 2.0 weights use the Tencent HY-WORLD 2.0 Community License** — commercial use is allowed **except in the EU, UK, and South Korea**, and orgs >1M MAU must get written Tencent approval ([License.txt](https://github.com/Tencent-Hunyuan/HY-World-2.0/blob/main/License.txt)). **Verdict:** the pick if we want to own the pipeline and run US-only under 1M MAU; the territory carve-out and GPU footprint are the governance flags.

### Blockade Labs Skybox AI *(cheapest, cleanest license, most kid-stylized)*
Input: text + style presets. Output: **360° equirectangular panorama (8K, 16K on Business), HDRI/EXR, depth map, optional 3D mesh, cube maps** ([site](https://www.blockadelabs.com/), [review](https://www.tooljunction.io/ai-tools/skybox-ai)). **License:** **"All plans include full commercial licensing"** with clear IP ownership — no FLUX/Street-View baggage; paid tiers $20–$112/mo ([plans](https://skybox.blockadelabs.com/plans)). **Runtime:** a skybox is the single cheapest 3D thing to render — a textured sphere on any Chromebook; the depth map/mesh export adds *mild* parallax but this is fundamentally **look-around, not walk-around**. **Look:** 48+ styles including cartoon/anime/painterly → excellent for our audience. **Verdict:** perfect for **backdrops, skies, and "stand-in-the-middle-and-look" zones**, and as the cheapest fallback for the weakest hardware; combine with hand-built foreground props to make a zone feel walkable (hybrid).

### Spline *(interactivity + lightest runtime)*
A **browser-native 3D design tool** with a real-time web runtime: export a Spline-Viewer, React component, or code, and it renders interactive 3D on modest hardware ([what is Spline](https://docs.spline.design/basics/what-is-spline)). **Spline AI** adds *object-level* text/image→3D, textures, and style transfer — **not full-scene world generation**. **License:** commercial use on paid plans ($12–$20/mo + $5 AI add-on); library assets are commercial-use ([pricing](https://spline.design/pricing)). **Look:** inherently stylized/clean — naturally kid-friendly and cheap to draw (meshes, not splats). **Verdict:** the best tool for the *interactive* half of "discovery zone" (clickable instruments, easels, editors, hover/press events) and the lightest runtime; pair it with a generated backdrop.

### WorldGen — ZiYang-xie *(open DIY, but fix the license first)*
Input: text or image → **3DGS (.ply) or mesh**, via panorama→scene ([repo](https://github.com/ZiYang-xie/WorldGen), [project](https://worldgen.github.io/)). **Hardware:** friendly — ~24 GB full, **~10 GB low-vram**, and **<8 GB** using nunchaku SVDQuant on a 16 GB card ([low-vram PR](https://github.com/ZiYang-xie/WorldGen/pull/7)); generates in seconds on a good GPU. **License:** the code is **Apache-2.0**, **but the default panorama stage is FLUX.1-dev, which is non-commercial** ([FLUX license](https://bfl.ai/legal/non-commercial-license-terms)) — a **commercial blocker as-shipped**. It becomes viable if we swap in **FLUX.1-schnell (Apache-2.0)** or buy a BFL commercial license. **Look:** any style. **Verdict:** the most attractive *self-hosted, low-VRAM* option **once the FLUX-dev dependency is removed**; output is a single 360° "bubble" per generation (room-sized zone), which suits a discrete discovery room.

### LayerPano3D *(research only)*
Text → **layered 3DGS panorama** with depth layers for exploration ([repo](https://github.com/3DTopia/LayerPano3D), [project](https://ys-imtech.github.io/projects/LayerPano3D/)). Code is Apache-2.0, but the released model is **FLUX.1-dev-based and fine-tuned on Google Street View → explicitly "non-commercial, personal or demonstration purposes only"** ([LoRA card](https://huggingface.co/ysmikey/Layerpano3D-FLUX-Panorama-LoRA)). It's effectively a research ancestor of WorldGen. **Verdict:** great ideas, **not usable in a product**.

### WonderWorld *(research-grade; connected worlds)*
Single image (+text, camera) → **Fast Layered Gaussian Surfels**, generating *connected, diverse* scenes in **<10 s each on an A6000 (48 GB)** ([project](https://kovenyu.com/wonderworld/), [paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.09394)). The "keep walking and it grows the world" model is compelling for exploration, but it's research code (license/commercial terms unclear, depends on a diffusion backbone), needs a 48 GB GPU, and surfels need conversion for standard splat viewers. **Verdict:** watch it; not a clean product pipeline today.

### Luma AI *(wrong tool for this brief)*
Now a **reality-capture** platform (phone photos/video → NeRF/3DGS/mesh) plus **Dream Machine** video; the text-to-3D **"Genie" was sunset Jan 1, 2026** ([capture app](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/luma-3d-capture/id1615849914)). Commercial rights exist on paid plans ([licensing](https://lumalabs.ai/learning-hub/licensing)). But it requires **real physical spaces** (conflicts with "synthetic only"), produces **photoreal** (not kid-stylized) output, and can capture real people. **Verdict:** excellent tech, poor fit for Interest Lab.

### CAT3D — Google DeepMind *(unavailable)*
Multi-view diffusion → NeRF/3DGS from a few images in ~1 min ([project](https://cat3d.github.io/), [paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.10314)). **No public weights/code**; Google-proprietary research. **Verdict:** reference only.

### Genie 3 / Oasis 3 *(the 2026 leaders — but a different paradigm)*
[Genie 3](https://deepmind.google/models/genie/) generates **real-time, promptable, photoreal worlds at 720p/24fps**, consistent for a few minutes — explicitly contrasted with "NeRFs and Gaussian Splatting" because it has **no explicit 3D representation**; it's autoregressive video on a server. [Oasis 3](https://decart.ai/oasis) is the same idea aimed at robotics/AV via API. **Verdict:** these are the *runtime*, and an expensive server-side one — they don't hand us a file to ship to a Chromebook. Out of scope for an offline asset pipeline, but worth tracking as the long-term "worlds on demand" horizon.

## Recommended pipeline (hybrid)

**Author offline → export a light asset → render in WebGL2 → add interactivity on top.**

1. **Environment shell (pick per need):**
   - *Turnkey / best quality:* **Marble** → export **compressed splat (SPZ/SOG)** for hero zones, **glTF mesh** for weak hardware.
   - *Own the pipeline / no per-asset cost:* **HY-World 2.0** (US-only, <1M MAU) → same exports.
   - *Cheap backdrop / weakest devices:* **Skybox AI** 360° pano (license-clean, renders anywhere).
2. **Runtime:** **three.js + [Spark](https://sparkjs.dev/)** (MIT) for splats; plain three.js/Babylon glTF for meshes; a textured sphere for skyboxes. Add **SuperSplat streamed LOD** if a zone gets large. Gate by device: serve mesh/skybox variants to low-end Chromebooks, splats to capable ones.
3. **Interactivity:** build the clickable discovery mechanics (instruments, easels, code terminals, hover/press/enter events) in **Spline** or directly in three.js, composited over the generated shell.
4. **Style control:** drive every generator with the same stylized reference art so zones share one warm, kid-friendly aesthetic; Skybox and Spline are stylized by default, Marble/HY-World need stylized image prompts.

**Generate-with-X vs hand-build vs hybrid:** pure generate-once gets us beauty fast but not interactivity or guaranteed Chromebook budgets; pure hand-build gives control but is slow and expensive to make beautiful. **Hybrid wins:** generators for the expensive, pretty backdrop; hand-built (Spline/three.js) for the cheap, interactive foreground and zone logic.
